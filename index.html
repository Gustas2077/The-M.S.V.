How it works

The Mandelbrot set is a set of complex numbers that are plotted on a complex plane. The set is defined by the following equation: zn+1 = zn2 + c

Where c is a complex number and z is a complex number that is part of the set. The set is defined by the complex numbers that do not diverge when the equation is iterated infinitely.

To visualize the set, the complex numbers are plotted on a complex plane. The real part of the complex number is the x coordinate and the imaginary part is the y coordinate. The color of the pixel indicates how many iterations it took for the complex number to diverge. The more iterations it takes, the darker the pixel.
